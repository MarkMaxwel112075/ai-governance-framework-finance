AI Governance Framework for Finance Teams

A practical AI governance framework for finance and BI teams adopting AI tools without a dedicated risk function, extending research from my MSc dissertation on AI in management accounting.

What this is

My dissertation found that AI tools only create real value in finance when they're backed by proper governance, not from the technology alone. This project takes that finding out of the academic space and turns it into something a finance team could actually start using: a maturity model, a focused risk checklist, and a phased rollout plan.

Frameworks applied


NIST AI Risk Management Framework (Govern / Map / Measure / Manage)
ISO/IEC 42001 (AI management systems standard)
EU AI Act risk tiers, checked against the current timeline as of mid-2026


What's inside


A 5-level AI governance maturity model, similar in structure to the maturity model I built for a separate commercial readiness project during my MSc
A risk taxonomy specific to finance use cases: model risk, data risk, explainability, vendor risk, automation bias, and operational resilience
A worked example showing how the risk levels and controls apply to real finance/BI tools
A phased 12-month implementation roadmap
An honest limitations section, since this was built as a self-directed project, not inside a real organisation
